## What it means to me…
Information equity and justice means considering not just the needs of everyone in the community but the interaction between the community and the library including the staff and external professional organizations. It is also important to assess how well the community is being served including the different components of the community and if some are better served than others. In both the case that the available resources at all levels are not being used and the case that certain members of the community are underserved, information equity and justice means working to ensure that these gaps can be filled. The library has a strong code of ethics but it is always important to question whether there are improvements including to the code of ethics themselves.
## How I have demonstrated this…
At the Wayland Free Library we analyzed the local community and found some existing inequality for non-native speakers, and I have also considered general inequality in the use of descriptive information in order to determine the appropriate metadata for describing collections. As part of my philosophy of librarianship I have questioned my own ethics as part of deciding to pursue librarianship, have interrogated the existing library resource ethics in terms of what is and isn’t available for free to the community and have considered the ethical use of digital formats by comparing WorldCat and online library reference resources. As part of working as a library clerk at the Wayland Library I support the library board policy that defends access to all information and the prevention of censorship and in my previous experience as a physics researcher I created policies for STEM outreach to young girls and high school students. Finally I created SCRLC Create, an Omeka archive that combines the resources of many different libraries in the South Central Regional Library Council to both connect myself, the users of our libraries, my colleagues and the members of the professional organization the South Central Regional Library Council but also to enable building online community for groups that are underserved in the physical space.
## What I plan to do in the future…
I will continue to work at the Wayland Free Library. STLS has many programs for addressing equity and justice. I will also join in ALA, NYLA and other professional organization working groups in order to bridge the gap between the resources at the local and at the professional level.
