## What it means to me…
Engaging and serving users and their communities means meeting them where they are. Even in a small community like the few thousand in the town of Wayland there are young and old, families who have lived there for generations and those new to the area, many different cultural and personal backgrounds. Librarians must both understand all these groups and their interactions with each other and also make sure to equally serve them all and prevent material related to any community from being suppressed.
## How I have demonstrated this…
As a library clerk at the Wayland Free Library I work with all ages providing technical support and also curating age appropriate materials in the form of middle grade graphic novels. Our library has also engaged with the non-English speaking and immigrant community in the form of a proposal to upgrade our web site and also a book series about the immigrant experience called “i’m your neighbor”. We traveled to the NYS Advocacy Day in order to prevent massive library funding cuts that would reduce the ability to support these and other communities. Also as part of my MSLIS work I have written about tools for supporting non-English speakers to create research proposals and discussed method to build educational tools to facilitate lifelong learning through libraries, work that I also had experience with as a post-doctoral researcher in physics when doing outreach with high school students and middle school girls.
## What I plan to do in the future…
In the future we will continue the work on our site to add non-English language support and intend to create online programming that can be inclusive of communities that are less present in the Wayland Library. We will create Facebook groups and then interview the moderators to understand the successes and failures of these efforts. I also intend to continue advancing my understanding of the available tools like Omeka for curating and storing data that is not prioritized for underserved communities.
